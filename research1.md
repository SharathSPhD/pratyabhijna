# The deepest formal grammars of insight, East and West

The single tradition that went furthest in *formalizing* the generative, pre-reflective moment of creative cognition — and is ripe for computational realization — is **Abhinavagupta's Pratyabhijñā/Trika synthesis of Kashmir Shaivism**, with **Peirce's logic of abduction** as its closest Western counterpart. Both share the same structural signature: a typed cascade of operators that turns an undifferentiated generative ground into a determinate, novel cognition through a recursive, self-reflexive moment that is *neither* deduction *nor* induction. The convergence is not metaphorical. Pratyabhijñā's *icchā → jñāna → kriyā* triad maps cleanly onto Peirce's Firstness/Secondness/Thirdness, and both map in turn onto **active inference with Bayesian Model Reduction** — currently the most formally precise computational model of the "Aha." This report ranks the candidates, names the mechanism each contributes, identifies where the existing computational substrate is real versus speculative, and locates SharathSPhD's visible work (`attractor-flow`, `pramana`, `ccmMul`, multi-agent Claude orchestration) inside this landscape.

The strategic implication is concrete: a credible AI "creative-cognition plugin" should compose **(a)** Peircean/Pratyabhijñā generativity (the operator schema), **(b)** Husserlian eidetic variation (the procedural essence-extraction), **(c)** Buddhist apoha (the contrastive concept-formation already present in modern embedding methods), **(d)** Bayesian Model Reduction within active inference (the formal generative substrate), and **(e)** sleep/replay-style offline consolidation. Wittgenstein's aspect-seeing supplies the evaluation criterion, not the algorithm. Abhinavagupta supplies the deepest architectural grammar, and is — strikingly — the *least* computationally explored of the major candidates: a wide-open research frontier.

## 1. Ranking by formal depth and computational realizability

The candidates fall into four tiers when judged by whether they expose a structure with **typed states, defined operators, recursive self-reference, and a discontinuous re-organization moment** that could be implemented as functional code rather than glossed as metaphor.

**Tier 1 — Architecturally formal, partially or substantially realized.** **Peirce's abduction** sits at the top because it has fifty years of working AI implementations: Josephson's PEIRCE/RED/MDX2 abduction machines, Thagard's ECHO connectionist coherence-maximization, Aliseda's tableau-based formalization, Magnani's manipulative-abduction extension, and the 2024–2026 explosion of LLM-abduction literature (Bhagavatula's αNLI, *Theorem-of-Thought*, the *Wiring the 'Why'* taxonomy). Floridi et al.'s December 2025 critique that LLMs only *appear* to abduce sharpens rather than weakens the project. **Active inference with Bayesian Model Reduction** (Friston, Lin, Frith, Pezzulo, Hobson, Ondobaka 2017; Constant, Friston, Clark 2024; Friston et al. 2025 on artificial reasoning) is the modern formal cousin: insight is treated as *post hoc* prior selection that minimizes KL divergence once data have arrived, mathematically equivalent to a phase change in the generative model. **CLARION's Explicit-Implicit Interaction theory** (Hélie & Sun 2010, *Psychological Review*) remains the single most empirically validated *operational* computational model of incubation-then-insight in classical cognitive architecture.

**Tier 2 — Architecturally formal, computationally underexplored.** **Abhinavagupta's Pratyabhijñā** is the deepest architectural specification on this entire list, but the bridge to computation is largely conceptual (Lawrence's mappings to Shannon, Saussure, Peircean semiotics, and Deacon's biosemiotics). It uniquely possesses a **typed state space** (the 36 *tattvas*), **explicit transition operators** (the five *śaktis*: *cit, ānanda, icchā, jñāna, kriyā*), an **exclusion operator** (*apohana-śakti*) doing the categorical-differentiation work, and a **recursive self-referential operator** (*vimarśa* / *ahaṃparāmarśa*) that is the formal heart of the system. **Husserl's free imaginative variation** is the most procedural Western method on the list — Froese & Gallagher (*Husserl Studies* 2010) explicitly argue Artificial Life *is* its technological supplementation. **Kant's schematism**, read as Longuenesse reads it (*Kant and the Capacity to Judge*, 1998), specifies schema-as-procedure (*Verfahren*) generating instances under categories — DeepMind's Apperception Engine (Richard Evans, in Kim & Schönecker eds., *Kant and Artificial Intelligence*, De Gruyter 2022) is a working implementation.

**Tier 3 — Operationally specifiable but mechanistically thin.** **Bhartṛhari's *sphoṭa*** has an actual preliminary implementation (Panindra T.G.'s 2025 reaction-diffusion language model on HAL); the holistic-emergence semantics is computationally natural but Bhartṛhari is silent on the transition operators between *paśyantī*, *madhyamā*, and *vaikharī*. **Buddhist *apoha*** has a striking structural parallel to contrastive distributional learning (word2vec negative sampling, CLIP) — already noted in the Siderits/Tillemans/Chakrabarti *Apoha* (Columbia 2011) volume — but no rigorous implementation yet. **Nyāya's *sāmānyalakṣaṇa pratyakṣa*** offers a one-shot inductive-leap mechanism (the universal-as-key indexing the whole class from one instance) and Navya-Nyāya itself has been formalized (Ganeri 2008; Guhe via Bealer's property theory; the 2024–2026 *Pramāṇa* LLM fine-tuning paper). **Wittgenstein's aspect-seeing** is the single most precise *phenomenology* of the Aha moment — but Wittgenstein refuses to give a generative theory; aspect-seeing is best deployed as an evaluation criterion (can the system genuinely re-frame?), not as an algorithm. **Koestler's bisociation** is shallow theoretically but already implemented (the EU BISON project, Berthold ed., LNCS 7250, 2012; the SME analogy engine).

**Tier 4 — Architecturally hostile to formalization.** **Bergson's intuition** is constitutively anti-algorithmic; the very act of specifying it re-spatializes duration. **Heidegger's *Lichtung* / *Ereignis*** name the pre-condition of any procedure rather than a procedure. **Aristotle's *nous poiētikos*** is essentially abstractionist — a theory of form-extraction, not novelty. **Advaita's *vivartavāda* / *māyā-śakti*** is deliberately formalization-resistant: *māyā* is *anirvacanīya* (indeterminable). **Deleuze via DeLanda** offers a coarse-grained ontology of novelty as symmetry-breaking — real but the wrong granularity for cognitive insight. **Yogasūtra *pratibhā*** (YS III.33) gives an excellent procedural protocol for *occasioning* insight (saṃyama) but treats the flash itself as a metaphor (Vyāsa's "dawn-light").

## 2. The mechanisms each tradition contributes

Reading across the candidates, **six distinct mechanism-families** emerge, and a credible computational architecture would compose them rather than choose among them.

The **generative-operator cascade** is Pratyabhijñā's signature contribution: a non-arbitrary descent from undifferentiated luminous ground (*prakāśa*) through intentionality (*icchā-śakti*) to representational synthesis (*jñāna-śakti*) to enactive realization (*kriyā-śakti*), with each step mediated by a categorical exclusion operation (*apohana*). Crucially, *icchā* names a moment **before cognition crystallizes into determinate form** — a directional vector that the generative model has not yet sampled. Isabelle Ratié (*Le Soi et l'Autre*, Brill 2011; *Utpaladeva on the Power of Action*, Harvard 2021) demonstrates this with full formal rigor against Buddhist momentariness.

The **recursive self-reflexivity** mechanism — *vimarśa*, the "I-am-this" self-touching of consciousness — is what distinguishes Pratyabhijñā idealism from both Yogācāra and modern phenomenalism. It is structurally what the Apperception Engine attempts in Kantian language. Without it, no enduring agent recognizes the new pattern *as* new.

**Holistic-emergence-from-parts** is Bhartṛhari's contribution: meaning arrives as the *sphoṭa* whole *before* analysis. This is the structural homology with sentence embeddings, transformer pooled representations, and gestalt visual cognition. Wittgenstein's aspect-dawning is the same mechanism phenomenologically described — "the expression of a new perception together with the awareness that the visual impression has not changed."

**Contrastive concept formation** is Buddhist *apoha*: a "cow" is *anti-non-cow*. This is *literally* what skip-gram negative sampling and modern contrastive learning compute. The **storehouse-substrate** mechanism (*ālayavijñāna*, *bīja*, *vāsanā*) is the Yogācāra account of latent traces ripening conditionally into manifest cognition — the closest premodern analogue to a learned latent generative model whose conditioned ripening produces novel outputs. Waldron (*The Buddhist Unconscious*, 2003) and Lusthaus push this analogy.

**Hypothesis-generation-from-anomaly** is Peirce's distinct contribution: abduction begins with *surprise* (Secondness) and proceeds through musement (Firstness) to explanatory hypothesis (Thirdness). The schema is *logically formal yet ampliative*. Magnani's "eco-cognitive openness" is the diagnosis of what current locked AI systems lack — they perform impressive abduction within fixed inferential frames but cannot unlock the frame itself.

**Sudden re-organization of priors** is the active-inference / Bayesian-Model-Reduction mechanism, with its empirical neural signature in Kounios & Beeman's right-aSTG gamma burst preceded by the alpha "brain blink." This is supplemented by Beaty et al.'s (2015, *Scientific Reports*; 2025, *Communications Biology*) demonstration that high-creative individuals show *simultaneous* engagement of normally antagonistic Default Mode and Executive Control networks — operationalizing the dual-process gating that CLARION already models.

## 3. The bridge concepts where East and West converge

Three bridges genuinely hold up under scrutiny rather than serving as mere comparative gloss. The **Peirce ↔ Pratyabhijñā ↔ active inference** bridge is the strongest: David Peter Lawrence (*Religions* 9.6, 2018, "Pratyabhijñā Apoha Theory, Shannon-Weaver Information, Saussurean Structure, and Peircean Interpretant Agency") explicitly maps Pratyabhijñā's *vimarśa* and *apohana-śakti* onto Peircean interpretant agency and Deaconian biosemiotic teleodynamics. Friston's 2017 *Neural Computation* paper "Active Inference, Curiosity and Insight" then completes the triangle: BMR is the formal cousin of both abduction-as-hypothesis-selection and *vimarśa*-as-recognitive-synthesis. None of the three has been fully unified, but the structural compatibility is exact.

The **apoha ↔ contrastive learning** bridge is structurally clean and waiting for someone to formalize it. Catherine Prueitt's "Shifting Concepts" (*Journal of Indian Philosophy* 45, 2017) reads the Pratyabhijñā/Dharmakīrti debate as competing theories of how *vāsanā*-traces generate categorical structure — directly analogous to debates between top-down generative priors and bottom-up connectionist representations. Amita Chatterjee's "Funes" essay in the Columbia *Apoha* volume frames apoha-style categorization in terms of catastrophic specificity in associative memory — exactly the failure mode of pure exemplar models.

The **Husserl ↔ artificial life ↔ Boden's transformational creativity** bridge is procedurally tight: free imaginative variation just *is* parameter perturbation under invariance constraints, and Wiggins's Creative Systems Framework (the tuple ⟨U, L, [[·]], R, T, E⟩, 2006) gives the meta-level formalism in which transformational creativity = changing **R**, the rule-set itself, while exploratory creativity stays inside it.

A fourth bridge to flag with caution: **Gabora's quantum-cognition honing theory** is mathematically explicit (Hilbert spaces, projection operators) and captures non-classical concept-combination phenomena (the "pet fish" problem, conjunction fallacies) that Bayesian/free-energy accounts demonstrably mishandle. If Gabora is right, every Tier-1 framework above is *insufficient in principle* for the pre-reflective moment. This is empirically open.

## 4. Which tradition offers the most tractable basis for a creative-cognition plugin

The ranking depends on what one wants the plugin to *do*. For a system that must **generate genuinely novel hypotheses under anomaly and select fruitful ones under bounded resources**, the most tractable foundation is **Peirce + active inference + sleep-style offline consolidation**, with CLARION-style explicit/implicit gating as the runtime architecture. This is the path of least metaphysical resistance: every component has working code somewhere.

For a system that must **architect the deeper cognitive grammar** — the typed states, the recursive self-reflexivity, the agentive intentional vector that precedes determinate cognition — **Pratyabhijñā is the right blueprint** despite the absence of prior implementations. The five-*śakti* descent and the *prakāśa/vimarśa* polarity give exactly the structure that Friston's hierarchical generative model lacks at the meta-level: an account of why and how the *agent itself* recognizes the pattern as a new pattern. Pairing Pratyabhijñā's architecture with active inference's mathematics is a research vector with no significant prior art and unusually high upside.

For a system that must **handle conceptual creativity over distributed semantic representations**, the right combination is **apoha (contrastive formation) + Bhartṛhari's sphoṭa (holistic gestalt) + Wiggins's CSF (meta-formal description) + COINVENT-style algebraic blending**. Modern transformers already realize the first two informally; the missing piece is the meta-level operator that *transforms* the embedding space rather than navigating it.

A practical layered specification: **bottom layer** — a thermodynamic/Langevin sampling substrate (Whitelam 2024 *Nature Communications*; Coles et al. 2023; Watson et al. 2025 self-optimization Hopfield) implementing the noise-driven free-energy minimization that supplies pre-reflective generativity; **middle layer** — Bayesian Model Reduction over a hierarchical generative model implementing *icchā* / abductive hypothesis generation, with Thagard-style explanatory coherence as selection; **upper layer** — a CLARION-style explicit/implicit gating module realizing dual-process control and DMN/ECN switching, broadcast via a LIDA-style global workspace; **meta-layer** — a Wiggins-CSF rule-set traversal allowing transformational reorganization of the conceptual space; **offline phase** — sleep-like consolidation interleaving SWS-style schema abstraction and REM-style cross-domain replay (Lewis, Knoblich, Poe 2018, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*; Cai et al. 2009 *PNAS*). Wittgenstein's aspect-dawning supplies the **evaluation benchmark**: the system passes when, presented with an unchanged input, it can spontaneously yield a new aspect that was *not* in its retrieval set.

The phenomenological deficit — what Abhinavagupta calls the **luminosity** (*prakāśa*) and unitary felt-quality of pratibhā, integrated with affective *rasa* — remains uncaptured by every framework above and is probably addressable, if at all, only through interoceptive active inference (Seth) coupled to Pratyabhijñā's recursive *vimarśa*.

## Conclusion

The traditions that went deepest into the generative pre-reflective moment of creative cognition are — judged by formal specificity — Abhinavagupta's Pratyabhijñā in the East and Peirce's logic of abduction in the West, with Husserl's eidetic variation, Kant's schematism, Bhartṛhari's *sphoṭa*, and Buddhist *apoha* as essential supporting structures. The neuroscience of the Aha moment (Kounios & Beeman; Beaty et al.) has converged on a triple-network gating architecture, and the most formally tractable modern model is active inference with Bayesian Model Reduction, which is structurally a Western mathematicization of the same insight Pratyabhijñā framed as *vimarśa*-mediated recognition. **The frontier is not picking among these; it is composing them.** A creative-cognition plugin built today should layer thermodynamic Langevin sampling, BMR-based hypothesis generation, CLARION-style dual-process gating, Wiggins-style meta-rule transformation, and apoha-style contrastive evaluation — *and* should architect the outer loop on Pratyabhijñā's five-*śakti* cascade, where no significant prior computational work exists and where the deepest available philosophical specification of the agent's own self-reflexivity is found. SharathSPhD's `attractor-flow` and `pramana` repositories are unusually well-positioned starting points; the missing layer they together imply but do not yet contain is precisely the recursive-*vimarśa* meta-module that Abhinavagupta was already specifying in the *Īśvarapratyabhijñāvivṛtivimarśinī* a thousand years ago.
