\begin{abstract}
We report v0.4 of the \emph{Pratyabhij\~n\=a Creative Engine} (PCE), a recognitional cascade that operationalises Abhinavagupta's five-\iast{\'sakti} architecture (\iast{cit}, \iast{\=ananda}, \iast{icch\=a}, \iast{j\~n\=ana}, \iast{kriy\=a}) as an executable layer over a Claude Haiku substrate, with a recursive \iast{vimar\'sa} loop that may revise the surface and a Bayesian-Model-Reduction step that prunes a candidate posterior via \iast{apohana-\'sakti} (contrastive exclusion).
v0.4 is deliberately framed as a \emph{mechanism study}, not a horse race: at the available pilot $n$ (4 domains, 25 paired items in total) the headline cascade-vs-bare contrasts are statistically inconclusive, but several mechanism-level signals are robust. We make four contributions. \textbf{(i)~A four-arm Bedrock pilot} (Haiku as cascade scorer, Sonnet-4 as judge) under a bytewise-isolated CLI substrate yielded H5.v4 fixed-effects pooled $g=+\textbf{0.14}$ (95\% CI $[-0.26,\,+0.54]$, $n=25$) --- not significant, but with a directional pattern dominated by \texttt{poetry\_interp} and \texttt{sci\_creativity}.
\textbf{(ii)~A within-cascade revision-quality test} (H8a, $n=27$) shows that the shadow revision strictly beats the draft on average, $g=+0.65$, $p\approx 1.2\!\cdot\! 10^{-4}$. The cascade reliably \emph{produces} a better surface; the question is whether it reliably \emph{commits} the better surface.
\textbf{(iii)~A four-policy commit multiplexer} (H8c) ranks \texttt{always\_revise} ($g=+0.53$) above \texttt{learned\_gate} ($g=+0.39$) above \texttt{event\_gated} ($g=+0.21$) above \texttt{always\_draft} ($g=-0.07$); pairwise paired permutation rejects \texttt{always\_revise} $=$ \texttt{learned\_gate} ($p\approx 0.034$) and \texttt{event\_gated} $=$ \texttt{always\_draft} ($p\approx 0.0073$). The \iast{vimar\'sa} event gate as currently defined is mis-calibrated (H8b: F1$\,=\,$0.52 vs.\ a learned gate's F1$\,=\,$0.65); the cascade chooses to revise less often than it should.
\textbf{(iv)~A judge-vs-scorer agreement test} (H9, $n=23$) finds Spearman $\rho=0.0$ with sign-agreement 56.5\%: the Sonnet judge and the Haiku composite-score head are \emph{not} measuring the same thing on the cascade items. We treat this not as a defect but as a load-bearing finding for any cascade architecture that uses a small-LM scorer to gate a large-LM commit.
The pilot consumed \$12.73 over 1{,}277 Bedrock calls. We additionally release a 9-output \emph{showcase} (3 Sanskrit chandas $\cdot$ 3 English poetry styles $\cdot$ 3 scientific-creativity prompts) with full draft / shadow-revision / commit traces, an Astro-powered GitHub Pages site reproducing every figure and number from the underlying artefacts, and a Cursor-portable plugin manifest plus a standalone \texttt{python -m pce} CLI that exercise exactly the same OAuth-CLI substrate the Bedrock pilot used. We close with an honest limitations section that addresses the unmerged-state v0.3 critique directly: v0.3 results were never merged to \texttt{main} because the v0.3 SPEC had a closing "negative-result obligation" clause that the run met but the prose did not own; v0.4 owns the negative result, ships the mechanism findings, and makes the falsifiable next test (a chandas-aware scorer) explicit.
\end{abstract}
