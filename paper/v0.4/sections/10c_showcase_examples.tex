\section{Showcase Examples}\label{sec:showcase}

The v0.4 release ships nine release-quality showcase outputs, three per stratum (Sanskrit chandas, English poetry, scientific creativity), each with a full draft / shadow-revision / commit trace and a per-domain validator output. The full set lives at \texttt{benchmarks/showcase\_v0.4/} (also rendered on the GitHub Pages site). Six of the nine items are real Phase 7 cascade traces; three (the Sanskrit chandas) are maintainer-curated reference compositions, validated by \texttt{tools/sanskrit\_chandas.py} but not yet generated by the cascade because the v0.4 scorer is not chandas-aware (\S\ref{sec:discussion}). This section walks each item briefly; the full traces are in the artefact directory.

\subsection{Sanskrit chandas (3 items, curated reference)}

\paragraph{anu\d{s}\d{t}ubh.} A 4$\times$8 \'sloka on the theme of \iast{vimar\'sa} (reflective recognition). The composition follows Utpaladeva's framing of \iast{prak\=a\'sa} (luminous ground) becoming \iast{vimar\'sa} (self-touching) when the perceiving self recognises itself as the perceiver. The chandas validator confirms a 32-syllable count.

\paragraph{g\=ayatr\={\i}.} A 3$\times$8 invocation to \iast{citi-\'sakti} as the witnessing ground (s\=ak\d{s}in). The composition follows Utpaladeva's framing of \iast{citi-\'sakti} as the substrate from which all cognition is illuminated. The validator confirms a 24-syllable count.

\paragraph{indravajr\=a.} A 4$\times$11 invocation in the canonical indravajr\=a metre. The validator's syllable count is within tolerance for the curated text (a known artifact of consonant-conjunct typography handled by the validator's documented tolerance, \texttt{tools/sanskrit\_chandas.py}); the v0.5 release will replace this with a live cascade output once a chandas-aware scorer is wired in.

\subsection{English poetry (3 items, real Phase 7 cascade traces)}

\paragraph{Imagist haiku (\texttt{p06}, gate worked).} A 5/7/5 imagist haiku (``White feather, iron rails / Sings as the train rushes through / Empty platform sleeps'') was the highest-composite Phase 7 output for \texttt{poetry\_gen} ($\sim\!0.635$). Honest reading: the \iast{vimar\'sa} event \emph{did not} fire on this item, so the cascade committed the draft. The shadow-revision pass produced ``Sings when trains pass through'' --- a slightly different image but the proxy scorer rates it $0.59$ vs.\ the draft's $0.64$, so the event-gate's decision to keep the draft was \emph{correct} by the scorer. This is the cascade's gate working as designed on an item where the draft was already good.

\paragraph{Dickinson slant (\texttt{p07}, gate miss).} A free-verse poem in the Dickinson tradition (slant rhyme, en-dash mid-thought, anaphora). The \iast{vimar\'sa} event did not fire, but the shadow revision (composite $0.567$) clearly beat the draft (composite $0.504$). This is exactly the calibration gap H8b documents: the event-gated commit policy under-fires on this domain. The H8c leaderboard's verdict that \texttt{always\_revise} out-performs \texttt{event\_gated} is driven by items like this.

\paragraph{Pastoral, traditional metre (\texttt{p01}, gate worked).} A traditional pastoral 5/7/5 with a rain-on-tin auditory image. \iast{Vimar\'sa} did not fire. The shadow revision tried to escalate ``sings'' $\to$ ``shrieks through'' and ``deepens to'' $\to$ ``devours the song'' --- a louder register. The proxy scorer rates the draft (0.566) above the revision (0.523), so the event-gate's decision to keep the draft was correct. The revision is shown side-by-side in the showcase for comparison; this is the cascade \emph{not} mistaking volume for craft.

\subsection{Scientific creativity (3 items, real Phase 7 cascade traces)}

\paragraph{Why mathematics works in physics (\texttt{s06}, vimar\'sa fired).} The gold-star case in the v0.4 release. Vimar\'sa fired (\texttt{vimarsa\_event=True}), the gate committed the revision, and the revision (composite 0.618) beat the draft (composite 0.587). The revision pass also converted the title from ``A Contrarian View'' to ``The Persistent Mystery'', reframing the essay from polemic to honest puzzle-statement.

\paragraph{Why ice floats --- beyond the textbook (\texttt{s02}, gate miss).} Vimar\'sa did not fire, so the cascade committed the draft (composite 0.535), but the shadow revision scored higher (0.593). The revision changed the title from ``Beyond the Textbook'' to ``Geometry and Thermodynamic Precarity'', a sharper framing that matches the brief. Items like this are the basis for H8c showing \texttt{always\_revise} outperforming \texttt{event\_gated}.

\paragraph{Why galaxies have spiral arms (\texttt{s05}, largest revision uplift).} The largest single-item shadow-revision uplift in the pilot (draft 0.486 $\to$ revision 0.595, $\Delta=+0.110$). Vimar\'sa did not fire so the cascade committed the draft; the shadow revision sharpened ``Framing 1 - The Traffic Jam'' to ``Framing 1 - Density Waves'', upgrading the analogy to its proper physics name (Lin--Shu density-wave theory). The revision is shown for comparison; the gold-star reading is that the cascade \emph{produced} the better surface but failed to commit it. The v0.5 ladder's learned-gate work is partly about catching items like this one.
