\begin{abstract}
We introduce the \emph{Pratyabhij\~n\=a Creative Engine} (PCE), a Claude Code plugin that operationalises Abhinavagupta's recognitional architecture (the five-\iast{\'sakti} cascade and the recursive \iast{vimar\'sa} loop) as an executable layer over an LLM, coupling each \iast{\'sakti}-operator to a concrete active-inference primitive: a temperature-tunable generative substrate (\iast{cit}), a constraint-aware aesthetic-coherence scoring head (\iast{\=ananda}), a candidate sampler (\iast{icch\=a}), an embedding-space contrastive exclusion operator (\iast{apohana}-driven \iast{j\~n\=ana}), free-energy posterior selection via Bayesian Model Reduction \citep{Friston2018BayesianModelReductionArxiv,FristonPenny2011}, surface enaction (\iast{kriy\=a}), and a recursive aspect-shift detector (\iast{vimar\'sa}). PCE additionally exposes a Hopfield-attractor \iast{\=alayavij\~n\=ana} with SWS/REM consolidation cycles. \textbf{This paper reports v0.3}, which directly addresses the four falsifiable findings of the v0.2 adversarial review: (i) a clean Haiku CLI substrate (subprocess flag isolation + scrubbed \texttt{HOME} + per-item leakage probe) eliminates Claude-Code-context contamination of the bare control; (ii) an event-gated commit policy with always-shadow revision lifts \iast{vimar\'sa} from prompting to a causal step on the \iast{j\~n\=ana} $\Delta F$ signal -- and lets us measure shadow-revision quality on \emph{every} item, fired or not; (iii) full active-inference uplift on the cascade causal path -- aspect-conditioned BMR (\iast{j\~n\=ana} now reduces over the right model space, not a constant prior), Hopfield \iast{\=alayavij\~n\=ana} as a warm-start aspect prior (\iast{apohana}), \iast{cit}-temperature plumbed through the \iast{icch\=a} fan-out posterior, and a per-item free-energy budget controlling abort/continue decisions; (iv) two new control arms isolate the architecture from confounds the v0.2 review flagged: \texttt{haiku\_bare\_2K\_scorer} (1 pass, $K' = 2K$ candidates) controls the \emph{extra-compute} confound, and \texttt{haiku\_generic\_revise\_2pass} (2 passes with a fixed generic creative-revise brief) controls the \emph{revision-protocol} confound. We re-run the v0.2 paired sample (n=20--30 across the same four domains) under the new four-arm matrix and pre-register eight hypotheses: H1.v3--H4.v3 are per-domain \texttt{haiku\_cascade} vs.\ \texttt{haiku\_bare}; H5.v3 is a random-effects DerSimonian--Laird pooled Hedges' $g$ across the four domains, replacing the v0.2 z-blend; \textbf{H6.v3 is the headline fairness contrast} (\texttt{haiku\_cascade} vs.\ \texttt{haiku\_bare\_2K\_scorer}, architecture-vs-more-compute); H7.v3 is \texttt{haiku\_cascade} vs.\ \texttt{haiku\_generic\_revise\_2pass} (architecture-vs-generic-2-pass), and H8.v3 is the within-cascade paired \texttt{score(revision)} -- \texttt{score(draft)} test on items the event-gated commit chose to revise. Length-controlled scoring (regression of word-count on per-pair composite) addresses the v0.2 review's worry that the cascade's longer surfaces alone might explain the win. All numerical claims in this paper are generated from \texttt{benchmarks/results\_v0.3/} and \texttt{audit/v0.3/} via \texttt{benchmarks/autoreport.py}. \textbf{Headline result.} {HEADLINE\_RESULT}\end{abstract}
