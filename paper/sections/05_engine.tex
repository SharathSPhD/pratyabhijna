\section{The Pratyabhij\~n\=a Creative Engine}\label{sec:engine}

The engine is a deterministic pipeline of seven operators plus two sleep-style consolidation cycles, all implemented in \texttt{src/pce/}. We summarise each operator's signature here and refer to Appendix~\ref{app:operator_specs} for the full equations and Appendix~\ref{app:consolidation} for the consolidation cycles.

\subsection{Substrate}
Two substrate primitives ground the cascade in real computation: a sentence-embedder $\phi_{\text{embed}}$ (\texttt{sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2}, $d=384$) and a generative LM. v0.3 supports two LM substrates behind a unified \texttt{GeneratorProtocol} interface: a local causal LM $\pi_{\text{LM}}$ (\texttt{Qwen/Qwen2-1.5B-Instruct}, autodetected device CUDA $\rightarrow$ MPS $\rightarrow$ CPU, \texttt{float16} where supported), and Anthropic's Claude Haiku via a \emph{clean CLI substrate}: every Haiku call is dispatched through \texttt{claude --print --tools "" --strict-mcp-config --disable-slash-commands --setting-sources='' --no-session-persistence} as a subprocess, run inside a scrubbed \texttt{HOME} that contains only the keychain symlink (macOS) or \texttt{.config/claude/} symlink (Linux) needed for OAuth. An explicit env allow-list (\texttt{ENV\_ALLOWLIST}) replaces any blind \texttt{os.environ} inheritance, and a per-process \texttt{IntegrityProbe} (\texttt{pce.substrate.integrity}) periodically asks the inner subprocess what plugins/skills/MCP tools it has access to and \texttt{LEAKAGE\_REGEX}-scans the response (negation-aware, so ``I have no plugins'' does not trigger). Sampling is deterministic given a seed under both substrates.

\subsection{Operators}

The cascade is a composition of seven typed operators. \textsc{Cit}, with signature $(\text{prompt}, \tau, \text{seed}) \to (\text{tokens}, \log p, \text{embedding})$, is a direct call to $\pi_{\text{LM}}.\text{generate}$; from v0.3 onward, the cit-temperature $\tau_{\text{cit}}$ is plumbed through to the \iast{icch\=a} fan-out posterior (below) so the substrate's exploration--exploitation knob actually reaches \iast{j\~n\=ana}'s evidence accumulator. \textsc{\=Ananda}, $(\text{candidate}, \text{constraint}, \text{retrieval-set}) \to r \in [0,1]$, returns a weighted aggregate of constraint-cosine, retrieval-set diversity, and form-fidelity heuristics (line- and syllable-count proxies for poetry forms). \textsc{Icch\=a}, $(\text{prompt}, K, \text{constraint}, \tau_{\text{cit}}) \to \{c_1, \dots, c_K\}$, produces $K$ candidate generations under a constraint-injected prompt; the parity sampler's $\tau_{\text{base}}$ is scaled by $\tau_{\text{cit}}$ and recorded on each candidate's \texttt{sampler} dict so the audit log captures the temperature used. \textsc{Apohana}, $(\{c_i\}, \text{must-avoid}, \text{Hopfield}) \to \{a_i\}$, returns a contrastive embedding score with an optional Hopfield warm-start: $a_i = 1 - \max_j \cos(\phi(c_i), \phi(\text{must-avoid}_j)) + w \cdot \text{HopfieldQuery}(\phi(c_i))$, where the Hopfield bonus reads a per-domain \iast{\=alayavij\~n\=ana} (\texttt{HopfieldStore} from \texttt{pce.active\_inference.hopfield}) under SWS or REM consolidation modes. \textsc{J\~n\=ana}, $(\{c_i, a_i\}, \text{aspects}, \pi_{\text{aspect}}) \to (i^*, \Delta F, p)$, adds an aspect-conditioned reduction target: when aspects are supplied, BMR reduces over the $|\text{aspects}|$ candidate models that boost in-support candidates by $1+\text{boost}$ while leaving out-of-support candidates at $1$, producing a non-degenerate $\Delta F$ that responds to aspect priors derived from the Hopfield warm-start, with the selected $i^*$ taken as the posterior maximum and $\Delta F$ recorded for the event-gated commit policy. \textsc{Kriy\=a}, $(c_{i^*}, \text{mode}) \to \text{surface}$, supports three modes --- \texttt{verbatim}, \texttt{polish} (deterministic detokenisation), and \texttt{claude\_polish} (Claude Code's editing surface, opt-in). Finally, \textsc{Vimar\'sa}, $(\text{surface}, \text{aspects}, \text{retrieval-set}, \Delta F) \to (\text{event}, \text{novelty}, \text{brief})$, is a causal step on the cascade: an event fires when $|\Delta F| \geq \texttt{DEFAULT\_DELTA\_F\_THRESHOLD}$ (default $0.01$) \emph{and} aspect-multiplicity is detected, in which case the cascade commits the revision; otherwise it commits the draft. A shadow revision is always generated and scored regardless of commit outcome, so H8a (revision-versus-draft within cascade) is measurable on every item, and the committed surface is then consolidated into the Hopfield store under either REM (append) or SWS (k-means abstraction) mode.

\subsection{Cascade}
The cascade in \texttt{src/pce/cascade.py} composes the seven operators in order. The state object \texttt{CascadeState} records \emph{both} \texttt{surface\_draft} and \texttt{surface\_revision}, a \texttt{committed} field (\texttt{"draft"} or \texttt{"revision"}), the \texttt{commit\_policy} that produced it (one of \texttt{"event\_gated"}, \texttt{"always\_revise"}, \texttt{"always\_draft"}), the per-item \texttt{FreeEnergyBudget} ledger, and every $\Delta F$ along the way. The cascade is deterministic: identical inputs and seed produce byte-identical outputs.

\subsection{Consolidation}
Two cycles operate over the Hopfield store: \textsc{ConsolidateSWS} (k-means with k chosen by elbow-statistic) for slow-wave-sleep abstraction; \textsc{ConsolidateREM} (Metropolis re-sampling that perturbs and re-stores) for rare-attractor maintenance. Both cycles are exposed as MCP tools so a downstream agent can request consolidation explicitly.
