\section{Results}\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Per-domain composites}

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/fig_per_domain_box.png}
  \caption{Per-domain composite scores. Each panel shows the three arms (no-PCE Haiku, no-PCE Local-Qwen, Local-Qwen + PCE) with item-level jittered points overlaid on the box. The within-domain spread reflects the per-item variability and is the substrate for the paired contrasts in Figure~\ref{fig:paired_deltas}.}
  \label{fig:per_domain_box}
\end{figure}

Figure~\ref{fig:per_domain_box} shows the raw composite distributions. Two qualitative observations are immediate: (a) the no-PCE Haiku arm has a large head-start in absolute composite on poetry\_gen and poetry\_interp because it is a much larger model; (b) on the AUT and sci\_creativity domains, where the scoring rewards multiplicity / non-obvious framings rather than fluency, the PCE arm narrows or closes the gap, and the local\_bare arm trails both. This pattern is consistent with the theoretical claim that the cascade contributes structural diversity rather than fluency.

Figure~\ref{fig:forest} summarises the four pre-registered tests: each marker is the paired mean $\Delta$ of (\texttt{local\_cascade} $-$ \texttt{claude\_haiku}) on the per-item composite; whiskers are 95\% BCa CIs; the panel-side annotations report Hedges' $g$ and the Holm-adjusted $p$. Detailed values are also reported in Table~\ref{tab:primary} and the autoreport block at the end of this section.

\subsection{Within-PCE event test (H6)}

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.7\linewidth]{figures/fig_h6_event_vs_no_event.png}
  \caption{H6: within-PCE composite for trials where the \iast{vimar\'sa} loop fired vs trials where it did not. This is the internal-validity contrast: if the recursive layer is causally responsible for any lift, fired trials should score higher.}
  \label{fig:h6}
\end{figure}

The within-PCE H6 test (Figure~\ref{fig:h6}) is a Mann--Whitney $U$ on the per-item composites partitioned by whether \iast{vimar\'sa} fired. Even when the headline H1--H4 contrasts are not directional, H6 isolates the recursive layer's contribution \emph{within} the PCE arm.

\subsection{Statistical power}

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.85\linewidth]{figures/fig_power.png}
  \caption{A-priori (under assumed $g=0.5$) vs retrospective (under observed $g$) power for H1--H4. The 0.80 threshold (red dotted) marks conventional adequacy; smaller-$n$ slices (sci\_creativity, AUT) are typically under-powered for the assumed effect size and we report this transparently.}
  \label{fig:power}
\end{figure}

Per-domain power (Figure~\ref{fig:power}) is computed under both the SPEC-pre-registered assumed effect size ($g=0.5$, the de-facto small-to-medium target in creativity benchmarking) and the observed retrospective $g$. Slices with $n<15$ are under-powered for $g=0.5$ and we flag this explicitly.

\subsection{Sensitivity vs Local-Qwen}

The sensitivity arm (\texttt{local\_cascade} vs \texttt{local\_bare}) appears in the autoreport JSON (\texttt{benchmarks/results/stats.json}, key \texttt{sensitivity\_vs\_local\_bare}) and isolates the PCE contribution from the LLM-substrate contribution. We report it because, under SPEC \S2's negative-result obligation, any directional lift PCE delivers \emph{against the same base model} is itself a positive finding even if the absolute Haiku contrast is not directional.
