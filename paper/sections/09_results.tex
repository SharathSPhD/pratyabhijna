\section{Results (v0.3 pilot)}\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Pilot scope and the v0.3 four-arm matrix}

The v0.3 pilot evaluates four arms across the same four creativity domains as v0.2:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{haiku\_bare}                  --- Anthropic Claude Haiku, single sampler call (no PCE). Architecture-vs-nothing baseline.
  \item \texttt{haiku\_cascade}               --- Haiku through the v0.3 event-gated, always-shadow-revision cascade with the active-inference uplift.
  \item \texttt{haiku\_bare\_2K\_scorer}      --- Haiku, single pass, $K' = 2K$ candidates fanned out by \iast{icch\=a}. \emph{Extra-compute control} (H6.v3).
  \item \texttt{haiku\_generic\_revise\_2pass} --- Haiku, 2 passes with \texttt{commit\_policy="always\_revise"} but a fixed generic creative-revise brief replacing the \iast{vimar\'sa}-generated brief. \emph{Revision-protocol control} (H7.v3).
\end{itemize}
Local-substrate arms are removed from the default matrix per the locked v0.3 scope (the v0.2 review's headline criticism was that the ``apples-to-apples'' v0.2 contrast still bundled cascade lift with extra compute, not that local-vs-Haiku needed re-running). Every Haiku call goes through the clean-substrate pipeline (subprocess flag isolation + scrubbed \texttt{HOME} + per-item \texttt{IntegrityProbe}; see \S\ref{sec:engine}); the boot probe and the per-item probes are recorded in \texttt{audit/v0.3/integrity\_probes.jsonl} and the pilot halts on any leakage unless \texttt{--allow-leakage} is set.

The pilot used $n{=}5$ items per domain, $K{=}3$ candidate fan-out per pass, max\_tokens$=200$, hard cost cap of \$20, and ran with \texttt{seed}=4242 throughout. All numerical claims below are auto-substituted from \texttt{benchmarks/results\_v0.3/stats.json} into Table~\ref{tab:primary} via \texttt{benchmarks/autoreport.py}; see \texttt{audit/v0.3/} for the raw run logs and per-item integrity probes.

\subsection{Length-controlled scoring}

The v0.2 review noted that the cascade's surfaces are systematically longer than \texttt{haiku\_bare}'s, and that mean-length alone could explain the win on length-correlated axes (POEMetric \texttt{lexical\_diversity}, AUT \texttt{creativity}). v0.3 \texttt{benchmarks/stats.py} now reports a per-pair length-controlled composite: the per-arm linear word-count effect is regressed out before pairing, and both the raw and length-controlled estimates and Hedges' $g$ values appear in the auto-report. If a hypothesis remains supported under the length-controlled estimate, the cascade's lift survives the length confound; if it does not, we report that explicitly.

\subsection{H5.v3 --- pooled effect across domains (random-effects DerSimonian--Laird)}

The v0.2 H5 ``z-blend'' aggregated per-domain $z$-scores under independence. The v0.2 review correctly noted this conflates magnitude and significance and is power-fragile. v0.3 replaces it with a random-effects DerSimonian--Laird meta-analysis of the per-domain Hedges' $g$ values (the same effect-size metric used in H1--H4): pooled $g$, $\tau^2$ (between-domain heterogeneity), and a 95\% CI from the random-effects standard error. This reads the cascade's lift as a single estimable quantity at the architecture level, with appropriate honesty about between-domain variability.

H6.v3 is the headline fairness test: per-domain paired \texttt{haiku\_cascade} $-$ \texttt{haiku\_bare\_2K\_scorer}. The 2K-scorer arm spends roughly the same number of Haiku-tokens as the cascade but on a single-pass $2K$-candidate fan-out scored by the same \iast{j\~n\=ana} BMR; if the cascade only ``works'' because it spends more compute, H6.v3 will be null or negative. If H6.v3 is positive on any domain, the architecture itself --- not the budget --- carries that domain.

\subsection{H7.v3 --- architecture vs.\ generic 2-pass}

H7.v3 is the protocol fairness test: per-domain paired \texttt{haiku\_cascade} $-$ \texttt{haiku\_generic\_revise\_2pass}. The generic-revise arm runs the same 2-pass pipeline but with the \iast{vimar\'sa}-generated brief replaced by a fixed generic creative-revise prompt. If H7.v3 is positive on any domain, the \emph{content} of the \iast{vimar\'sa} brief --- not the mere existence of a revision pass --- carries that domain.

\subsection{H8.v3 --- within-cascade revision-vs-draft (causal contribution of the second pass)}

H8.v3 directly tests the causal claim of the v0.3 cascade: for items where the event-gated commit chose to commit revision, does the revision text actually score higher than the shadow draft? We pair $\texttt{score(surface\_revision)} - \texttt{score(surface\_draft)}$ within \texttt{haiku\_cascade} on every item with \texttt{committed=="revision"}, score both with the same domain scorer, and report the paired Hedges' $g$, BCa CI, and exact sign-flip permutation $p$. Because the cascade always produces a shadow revision regardless of commit policy (ADR-002), the test is well-defined for every \texttt{committed=="revision"} item.
