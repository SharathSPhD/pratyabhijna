\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}

The pre-registered design forces us to resist post-hoc storytelling. We discuss only the patterns that the data already exhibit, including the patterns that contradict the v0.2 paper's claims.

\paragraph{The v0.3 pilot is a directional-negative replication.} On all four primary domains, the \texttt{haiku\_cascade} arm trailed the clean-substrate \texttt{haiku\_bare} arm (Hedges' $g \in [-0.90, -0.22]$); the random-effects-pooled effect was $g=-0.46$ (95\% CI $[-0.93, +0.01]$). H6.v3 (vs.\ a budget-matched 2K-scorer) and H7.v3 (vs.\ a generic 2-pass revise) likewise failed to support the cascade on any domain. The within-cascade H8.v3 paired test came out directionally positive ($\Delta=+0.004$, $g=+0.21$) but only on $n{=}3$ items where event-gated commit selected revision, far too few to establish causality. The pilot is consistent with the v0.2 adversarial review's specific prediction --- that the v0.2 ``cascade beats bare'' lift was at least partly attributable to (a) Claude-Code-context contamination of the bare control, and (b) extra compute on the cascade arm that the design did not match against. With both confounds eliminated, the lift does not reproduce.

\paragraph{What the v0.3 architectural changes \emph{did} accomplish.} While the \emph{outcome} is null/negative, several v0.3 \emph{methodological} contributions are independently verifiable from the audit log:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
  \item \textbf{Clean substrate works as advertised (ADR-001).} 22 of 22 per-item integrity probes passed (\texttt{audit/v0.3/integrity\_probes.jsonl}); no Haiku output triggered the negation-aware \texttt{LEAKAGE\_REGEX}; the outer-host smoke test confirms PCE plugins still load in the host while the inner subprocess is hermetic. The substrate isolation is the v0.3 contribution that survives the negative pilot.
  \item \textbf{Event-gated commit + always-shadow revision works as designed (ADR-002).} On the 20 cascade items, the event-gated commit selected revision on $n=3$ (15\%) and draft on $n=17$ (85\%), with revision concentrated in the multiplicity-rewarding \texttt{poetry\_interp} domain (2 of 5) and \texttt{sci\_creativity} domain (1 of 5). H8.v3 is therefore measurable on every item via the always-shadow revision, even when commit chose draft. This addresses the v0.2 review's ``H6 is undefined when there's no event-vs-no-event split'' criticism.
  \item \textbf{BMR aspect-conditioned reductions are non-degenerate.} The Phase-3 gate confirmed $|\Delta F| \geq 0.01$ on the duck-rabbit textual probe (\texttt{audit/v0\_3\_phase3\_gate.json}), validating the aspect-conditioned soft-boost reduction strategy.
  \item \textbf{Active-inference plumbing reaches the causal path.} \texttt{cit\_temperature} now modulates \iast{icch\=a}'s sampler-grid posterior, the Hopfield store contributes a warm-start prior to \iast{apohana}, and the per-item free-energy budget gates abort/continue decisions. The Phase-4 gate confirmed \texttt{revision\_differs\_from\_draft} $\geq 80\%$ on prove-gate items (\texttt{audit/v0\_3\_phase4\_gate.json}).
\end{itemize}
The negative pilot result therefore does \emph{not} retract the v0.3 substrate, gating, or active-inference contributions; it constrains what those contributions can claim about composite-score lift on this benchmark sample at this $n$.

\paragraph{Why the cascade may have lost on this pilot.} Several specific mechanisms are consistent with the data:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em]
  \item The cascade's \iast{j\~n\=ana} BMR step \emph{selects} from $K{=}3$ candidates, while the H6.v3 control's $2K$-scorer step selects from $K'{=}6$. With $K$ small, the larger candidate pool is doing more work than the architecture.
  \item The cascade's revision pass, when it fires (15\% of items), is constrained by the \iast{vimar\'sa} brief which is itself derived from a small evidence set ($K{=}3$ \iast{apohana}-scored candidates). H7.v3's generic 2-pass revise is unconstrained and may be exploring a wider rewrite manifold.
  \item Length-controlled scoring (regression of word-count on per-pair composite) does not flip the sign, so the cascade is not trailing because of a length confound; it is trailing on within-arm-length-controlled composite as well.
  \item The Haiku substrate at $n{=}5$/domain may simply be too compact for the cascade's structural advantage to clear noise. The v0.4 falsification would raise $n$ to 20--30/domain at $K{=}4$, raise event-gating $\Delta F$ threshold so revision fires more often, and re-test the same hypotheses.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{What the engine does \emph{not} claim.} PCE does not claim to replicate phenomenological consciousness, and on the v0.3 pilot it does not claim to beat its budget-matched or protocol-matched controls on apples-to-apples score. The Pratyabhij\~n\=a vocabulary is borrowed because its taxonomy is exceptionally fine-grained, not because we make first-person claims. Likewise, BMR is a posterior-switching primitive in our system; we do not claim the Friston-style free-energy variational identity holds at the system level. The honest v0.3 claim is: \emph{the v0.3 plugin operationalises a clean Haiku CLI substrate, an event-gated causal vimar\'sa, and a falsifiable active-inference uplift; those mechanisms work as designed; and on this $n{=}5$/domain pilot the resulting cascade does not exceed its budget-matched or protocol-matched controls. The negative finding is reported in compliance with the SPEC's negative-result obligation and is, by construction, falsifiable by a properly powered re-run with the parameter changes catalogued in \S\ref{sec:limitations}.}
