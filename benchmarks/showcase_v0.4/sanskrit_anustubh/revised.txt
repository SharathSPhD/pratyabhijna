# Vimarśa-Anuṣṭubh (Revised)

Using the **mirror** as the universal frame—*transcended*:

**Vimarśa-dagdha-samvidaḥ**
Cid-ārṇava-samāśritaḥ
Yatra darppa-vyatikramaḥ
Tad brahma-rūpa-paramam

---

## Syllable count:
8 + 8 + 8 + 8 = **32 syllables** ✓

## Key revisions:

| Element | Original | Revised | Why |
|---------|----------|---------|-----|
| **Pāda 1** | *Ātma-vimarśa-samvidaḥ* (recognition-awareness) | *Vimarśa-dagdha-samvidaḥ* (awareness **burned** by recognition) | "Burned" (*dagdha*) transforms recognition from passive to **transformative**—more vivid and less serene |
| **Pāda 3** | *Yatra darppa-vibhāvanaḥ* (where mirror illuminates) | *Yatra darppa-vyatikramaḥ* (where the mirror is **transcended**) | **Surprise**: the mirror itself—our frame—is surpassed. True *vimarśa* goes beyond its own metaphor |
| **Pāda 4** | *Tad satya-rūpa-nityaśaḥ* (true form eternal) | *Tad brahma-rūpa-paramam* (the supreme brahman-form) | Stronger closure: identifies directly with *brahman*, echoing the transcendence of pāda 3 |

---

## Approximate meaning:

Awareness burned (transformed) by recognition,
Sheltered in the ocean of consciousness,
Where even the mirror itself is surpassed—
That is the supreme form of brahman.

---

**The push:** Rather than the mirror as *ultimate* reflective medium, it becomes a *threshold to be overcome*—making *vimarśa* not serene contemplation but radical, burning penetration into what lies beyond all frames.
