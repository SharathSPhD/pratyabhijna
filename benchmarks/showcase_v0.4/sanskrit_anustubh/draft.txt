# Vimarśa-Anuṣṭubh

Using the **mirror** as the universal frame:

**Ātma-vimarśa-samvidaḥ**
Cid-ārṇava-samāśritaḥ
Yatra darppa-vibhāvanaḥ
Tad satya-rūpa-nityaśaḥ

---

## Syllable count: 
8 + 8 + 8 + 8 = **32 syllables** ✓

## Approximate meaning:

The self-recognizing awareness,
Taken refuge in the ocean of consciousness,
Where the mirror illuminates—
That is the true form eternal.

---

**Note:** The verse uses *darppa* (mirror) as the abstract universal frame through which *vimarśa* (reflective recognition) manifests. The four pāda follow anuṣṭubh meter, with consciousness itself as both the reflecting medium and that which is reflected.
