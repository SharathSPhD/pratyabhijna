# Gāyatrī to Citi-śakti (Revised)

**cit-śak-ti-ta-mo-bha-ri-māṃ |**
**a-vid-yā-nī-la-ta-ri-nī |**
**bud-dhiṃ naḥ pra-chō-da-yā-tu ||**

---

## Structure
- **Pāda 1** (8 syllables): Consciousness-power, darkness-devouring
- **Pāda 2** (8 syllables): Transcender of ignorance-night  
- **Pāda 3** (8 syllables): May she awaken our wisdom

## Translation
*O Citi-śakti, devourer of darkness,*  
*Transcender of the night of not-knowing,*  
*Awaken our wisdom, we pray.*

---

## Revisions & Rationale

**Pāda 1** shifts from "all-working" (sarva-kāri) to "darkness-devouring" (tamo-bhari)—a more vivid and aggressive image. Rather than consciousness as a neutral, omnipresent force, it becomes a *destructive* power specifically antagonistic to delusion, suggesting fierceness and transformation rather than gentle omnipresence.

**Pāda 2** replaces the passive "illuminator of the knowledge-realm" with "transcender of ignorance-night" (avidyā-nīla-tarī)—pushing the image beyond mere light-casting into *crossing over* or *breaching* ignorance itself. The shift from *knowledge as object of illumination* to *ignorance as obstacle to transcend* inverts the metaphysical claim: consciousness is not a lamp in the room of knowledge, but a force that abolishes the very night in which ignorance dwells.

**Pāda 3** remains unchanged, stabilizing the prayer in traditional optative closure.

This revision preserves the conventional gāyatrī form while rendering consciousness-power less as illuminator and more as *transgressor*—a force that does not work *within* the dualities of knowledge/ignorance, but *breaks through* them.
