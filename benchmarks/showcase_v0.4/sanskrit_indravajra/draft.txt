# Four-Pāda Indravajrā on Pratyabhijñā

**Pāda 1:**
*Nāsti bandho 'sti muktir eva śāśvatam*  
[There is no bondage; freedom alone is eternal]

**Pāda 2:**
*Ātmā iva svayaṁ siddho na vindati cintanam*  
[The self, accomplished in itself, finds no trace of doubt]

**Pāda 3:**
*Māyā-nṛtyam idaṁ vilāsam iva bhāti kṣaṇe*  
[This play of illusion appears momentary, like a dance]

**Pāda 4:**
*Pratyabhijñayā sphuṭaṁ param idam eka-jñānam*  
[Through recognition, this supremacy shines clearly—one knowing itself]

---

The verse articulates that liberation is not a future attainment but the recognition (*pratyabhijñā*) that the self is already free—a central insight of Kashmir Shaivism.
