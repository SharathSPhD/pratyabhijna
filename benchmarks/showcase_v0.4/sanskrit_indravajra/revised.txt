# Four-Pāda Indravajrā on Pratyabhijñā—Revised

---

## Revision Notes

The revised version deepens the paradox at the heart of *pratyabhijñā*: that bondage is not freedom's opposite but rather freedom's mechanism of self-revelation. I have pushed **Pāda 3** further, making the chains themselves the site of recognition rather than merely illustrative of illusory appearance. The concrete image—a bound figure in movement—becomes paradoxical: the very resistance that seems to confine becomes the threshold where consciousness witnesses itself.

---

## Revised Verses

**Pāda 1:**
*Spandanam eva bandhāyāṁ—muktaḥ spandati śāśvatam*  
[Movement itself in bondage—the liberated one moves eternally]

**Pāda 2:**
*Rajju-pratikriyāyāṁ śakti—na samvedanā na bhaya*  
[In rope-resistance, power—neither sensation nor fear appears]

**Pāda 3:**
*Hasta-pāda-gatiḥ khalv-eva—śṛṅkhalam eva cit-sākṣat*  
[Hands and feet moving—the chains themselves shine forth as consciousness witnessing]

**Pāda 4:**
*Pratyabhijñā-kṣaṇe tatra—bandho 'pi mukti-prakāśanam*  
[In the moment of recognition there, even bondage reveals itself as liberation's radiance]

---

## Sharpened Claim

Where the baseline reads bondage as mere illusion to be transcended, this revision suggests something stranger: that the chain itself—the very resistance encountered—is the substance through which consciousness recognizes its own freedom. Bondage becomes not what obscures liberation but what manifests it.
